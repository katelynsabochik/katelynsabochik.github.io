Coffee Bean Types

Which coffee beans are right for you?

Bean Types

Arbica

More than 70% of the world's coffee is Arabica beans. While Arabica coffee is harder to grow and more expensive to buy, most people prefer the taste compared to Robusta. It also has less caffiene that Robusta coffee.

Go buy some.

Robusta

Robusta coffee makes up a small portion of the world's coffee production - and for good reason. Some people think Robusta coffee tastes like burnt tires (yum!). That said, Robusta is cheaper and has more caffiene. So if you're looking for a quick, cheap, caffiene fix. Robusta might be for you.

Go buy some. I guess, I mean if you really want to.

Roasting Methods

There are lots of ways to roast coffee. Find out which one is right for you based on flavor profile and caffeine content.

Unroasted

Don't buy these. You'll have to roast them before you can use them and who has time for that? Unroasted coffee beans are essentially raw or they have been dried as the first step in the roasting process. Unless you know how to roast coffee beans already (in which case, why are you looking at this site?) just walk away.

Go buy some. Good luck.

Light Roast

Light roast taste like toasted grains. With a light roast, you can better taste the origin of the coffee bean. They are more acidic than medium or dark roasts. They also have the most caffiene.

Go buy some.

Medium Roast

Medium roasts are more balanced in flavor and less acidic than a light roast. If you don't know what you like, just start with a medium roast. You'll be fine.

Go buy some.

Dark Roast

Dark roasts have an oily finish on the surface of the beans. Generally the taste of the beans is overshadowed by the taste of the roasting process. Dark roasts can taste sort of burned AND there's significantly less caffiene. So if that's your thing, go for it.

Go buy some.
